Do We Really Need to Copy the West to Make an Impact in Today's World ?




Many of us as Muslims, Arabs, or Africans assume that our race, religion, and history is not enough to cause a change or impact in this world. Haven’t you ever thought about why so many youth coming from the diaspora believe that we cannot cause an impact? We’re constantly scrolling through feeds filled with Western glamour, thinking maybe we have to copy their style, their mindset, or even ditch parts of our faith just to “make it.”
- LAYAN ALI ELNOUR(Sudan)
Our ancestors, our fathers, mothers, grandparents, and great-grandparents valued and respected their heritage. They didn’t chase the West to become the best. They stood firm on their cultural and Islamic principles and reached heights nobody in their time could touch. Look at our history. Take Al-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam, a legendary companion of the Prophet (Peace Be Upon Him). At just 15 years old, he became known as the “Sword of the Faith.” His bravery showed what young belief can achieve. Or think about Ibn Sina, often called Avicenna. He started studying medicine at 13 and became a skilled physician by 17. His Canon of Medicine became one of the most important medical texts for centuries. Then there is Ibn al-Haytham, who shaped optics, mathematics, and science itself, changing how the world understands light and vision. These are not just stories, they are proof that youth can change the world without losing identity. Fast forward to today, and we still see it happening. Ibrahim Alkurdi influences youth through faith, character, and purpose. Bahja Abdi empowers young girls and women with strong mindset, productivity, and identity. Mariam Solika founded “The Good News” platform at just 21, now reaching over 20 million people monthly, reshaping narratives about Arab and Muslim youth. In sports and activism, Bilqis Abdul-Qaadir and Dalilah Muhammad represent excellence while holding onto their Black Muslim identity. All these examples show one thing clearly: success and identity can exist together.

Western culture will try to convince us that our background is a limitation. It pushes individualism, materialism, and trends that fade fast. But our heritage gives us depth, purpose, and real power. Our ancestors built civilizations, advanced science, preserved faith through trials, and raised generations on principles of justice and knowledge. We don’t need to imitate impact we need to innovate from our roots. So let’s stop doubting. Start studying our history, building skills, creating content, launching businesses, and leading with faith. Our time is now. We are enough. We are the change.
